You do not need to donate to join ISKCON. It is open and free for anyone to join. Essentially what ISKCON represents is an ideal, a way of life and a revival of an ancient spiritual culture. So “joining” ISKCON means to adopt their practices and incorporate them into your life. The main practices are regularly chanting the holy name of God (Krishna) and eating holy food (prasadam).

What is mission of ISKCON?

This is the mission of ISKCON as per Srila Prabhupada the founder acharya.

  1. To systematically propagate spiritual knowledge to society at large and to educate all people in the techniques of spiritual life in order to check the imbalance of values in life and to achieve real unity and peace in the world.
  2. To propagate a consciousness of Krishna (God), as it is revealed in the great scriptures of India, Bhagavad-gita and Srimad-Bhagavatam.
  3. To bring the members of the Society together with each other and nearer to Krishna, the prime entity, thus developing the idea within the members, and humanity at large, that each soul is part and parcel of the quality of Godhead (Krishna).
  4. To teach and encourage the sankirtana movement, congregational chanting of the holy name of God, as revealed in the teachings of Lord Sri Caitanya Mahaprabhu.
  5. To erect for the members and for society at large a holy place of transcendental pastimes dedicated to the personality of Krishna.
  6. To bring the members closer together for the purpose of teaching a simpler, more natural way of life.
  7. With a view towards achieving the aforementioned purposes, to publish and distribute periodicals, magazines, books and other writings.

How can I contribute to ISKCON?

By dedicating your life, by contributing money, by using your intelligence or by spreading the word of Prabhupada.

Guidelines for Joining

Chanting 16 rounds Hare Krishna Mantra daily.

Follow the 4 regulative principles (No meat eating, no gambling, no intoxication and no illicit sex)

Also, you could join devotee training centers where you will get the first-hand experience of Vaishnava culture followed in temples. Then according to your inclination, the devotees concerned would guide you if you are fit for ashram life or grihastha life outside the temple.

Motivation to join Hare Krishna movement

One has to read Prabhupadas books especially ‘Bhagwad Gita’ and ‘Bhagwatam’ to get convinced about joining. Most importantly one must have a missionary spirit to help spread the movement of Chaitanya Mahaprabhu and Srila Prabhupada. Recommend you to read “Prabhupada Lilamrita” to get to know more about Prabhupada and his teachings.

All core members of Iskcon have to take vows in front of witnesses to abstain from the following activities for the rest of their lives:-

Eating meat, fish and eggs.

Taking intoxicants or stimulants of any kind, including tea and coffee.

Having illicit sex. Which is defined as any sex that is not with your wife for progeny.

Gambling. This includes buying lottery tickets or playing bingo.

The vows are taken solemnly after a period of observation by the guru or his representative to make sure that the initiate is sincere. The time varies from 6 months to several years. If the candidate is not considered trustworthy and sincere, then they will not receive initiation.

These vows are to protect and foster the qualities of austerity, compassion, cleanliness and truthfulness.

Because of these vows and the other spiritual practices that each initiate agrees to follow, there exists a society of men, women and children who are vegetarian, never get drunk, stoned or under the influence of any mind-altering substances. They never waste money gambling in the hopes of getting something without working honestly for it, and they are not driven by sex-desire.

Joining Iskcon is not a career move. The higher you rise, the more renounced you are expected to be. Sannyasis do not own property, do not have salaries or wages, do not have girl-friends or mistresses. They travel at the behest of the temples and devotees and stay in accommodation arranged and paid for by others. It may be a 5 star hotel in Dubai, or it may be a grass hut in a village in Bangladesh.

As a registered charity Iskcon is subject to yearly auditing and financial scrutiny from the government. All donations are to be issued receipts. In the temples I have been associated with, the Hundi donation box is opened with three officers of the temple present, and now it is done in camera, and the money counted and totaled with minimum of three officers present.

Large donations over 3 lacs have to be accompanied by a form with the donor’s name and address and statement of purpose for the donation filled out by the donor.

No single person has control over the policies of Iskcon. At the top decisions are made collectively by the GBC which numbers over 40 people including the deputies and ladies, from countries all around the world.

No single person in any temple has control over finances. Two signatories are always required.

No Iskcon member can appropriate funds for their own personal use without sanction from the relevant authorities after requesting permission.

No Iskcon member can deposit donations into their own private personal account.

All sannyasis are required to furnish details of all the donations that they receive and the expenses that they have incurred for the year, to the sannyas ministry. Many Sannyasis make the information public so that individuals who gave donations can see that they are accounted for.

Sometimes a devotee needs a hand with some expenses maybe building a temple, making repairs after a hurricane, putting on a festival, having a kidney transplant, the list is huge because it’s a dynamic society where a lot is going on and there are an ever increasing number of devotees. Some devotees have well-paying jobs and some are entirely dependent on a modest stipend from the temple.

The point to understand is that devotees ask (there is no force) for a donation (something that is given voluntarily). Sometimes the request is general to all devotees, as in the case of the TOVP, sometimes it’s within a temple community for a specific celebration, and sometimes it’s one on one. Everyone can decide if they want to contribute something, how much, and for what.
